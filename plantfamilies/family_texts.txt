(Boraginaceae: scorpiod or helicoid cyme, hispid aka bristly leaves, 2 carpels 4 nutlets, corona aka internal appendage of corolla)
(Hydrophyllaceae: helicoid cyme, anthers exserted longly, style often divided in two)
(Polemoniaceae: 5n floral parts, stamen alternate to petals, hyaline calyx alien translucent thing, simple to compound leaves, 3 forked fused carpel, NO bracteoles!)
(Ericaceae: rhododendron and huckleberry, urn shaped flowers, 10 poricidal or horned stamen, mycorrhizal)
(Cornaceae: opp simple entire leaves, 4 showy bracts posing as petals, terminal cluster inflorescense)
(Montiaceae: sorta succulent herbs, simple leaves, 2 bracteoles under flowers, sometimes ghastly/fluttery looking)
(Cactaceae: succulent herbs and shrubs, spiny, inferior ovary, infinity stamen and tepals)
(Caryophyllaceae: blade and claw stamen can pose as petals, notched petals, swollen nodes, superior ovary)
(Fabaceae: pinnately palmately ternately compound leaves, brush flower or banner flag flower, 10 or infinity stamen)
(Salicaceae: willow n poplar, trees or shrubs, catkins only one sex per plant, dentate to serrate leaves with distinct small edgehairs, hairy bract)
(Sapindaceae: trees, opposite leaves, nectar disk, palmately lobed leaves)
(Ranunculaceae: follicle or achene fruit, 5n herbaceous, various leaves, actino or zygo)
(Fagaceae: oaks, leaves from simple to lobed, nut or capsule, monoecious catkins aka 1-3 female flowers combined with male catkin)
(Betulaceae: birch, doubly serrate leaves, monoecious but distinct catkins for each sex, flowers sometimes have showy leaflike bracts)
(Berberidaceae: oregon grape, 6n whorls of 3 or rarely just 4, 1 carpel, valvate anthers)
(Lauraceae: 3n floral parts occ 4, nectar blobs, valvate anthers like bear paw with 4 flaps, leathers pale dark green leaves)
(Rosaceae: strawberry and peach, stipules at leaf base, pinnately palmately compound leaves, hypanthium, 5n, 1/5/inf carpels)
(Brassicaceae: 4n, often basal rosette, cross shape, silique silicle, 6 stamen 2 shorter)
(Violaceae: cordate leaves, basal rosette, zygomorphic, nectar spur, bell flowers)
(Saxifrigaceae: basal rosette, alt leaves, 5n flowers, zygomorphic, peltate aka umbrella leaves, conelike inflorescense, hypanthium, hair, 2 carpels fused at base, often in distinct thin tall shape or w big leaves)
(Magnoliaceae: spiral flowers, infinity, laminar stamen like paddles, alt simple leaves)
(Asteraceae: sunflowers, herbs/shrubs/trees, 5 petals/stamens, nectar pump, capsule, ray$ or disk* flowers)
(Apiaceae: carrots, herbs, various leaves w sheating bases, hollow stems, umbel aka upside down umbrella inflorescence, 5 sepals/petals/stamens, schizocarp fruit w 2 halves attached at tip)
(Caprifoliaceae: honeysuckle, $, 4top1bottom or 2top3bottom petals, inferior ovary, 2-5 fused carpels, herbs small trees or lianas)
(Plantaginaceae: snapdragon, capsule fruit, herbs or shrubs, $ bilabiate, varying lengths 4 stamen, opp/alt simple leaves sometimes in a distinct herby pattern, 4/5 sepals/petals connate)
(Lamiaceae: mint, herbs/shrubs/trees, herb stems square, opp decussate leaves aka 90deg rotations, bilabiate flower aka 2-lipped tubular, varying lengths 4 stamen, 4 nutlets/drupe)
(Liliaceae: trifolium, herbs, 6 showy tepals, 6 stamen, 3 connate carpels, alt/whorled/rosette simple leaves with parallel venation, berry or capsule fruit)
(Iridaceae: herbs, simple leaves equitant/2-ranked aka spiky basal, 6 tepals, 3 stamen, 3 connate carpels, capsule fruit)
(Araceae: terrestrial/aquatic herbs, alt simple leaves, spadix aka fleshy spike inflorescence with showy bract, berry)
(Orchidaceae: terrestrial mycotrophic herbs, aerial roots, simple leaves parallel veining often thick/waxy, zygomorphic flowers, lowers tepals divided into frilly labellum)
(Poaceae: rice! bamboo!, hollow stem, sheathing leaves, bends to group at joint-like nodes, spikelet often opening-up-like or airy)
(Cyperaceae: triangular stems, distinct terminal small-medium dense spikelet inflorescence but it various arrangements)
(Juncaceae: actinomorphic 6n flowers! most commonly flowerlike out of all grasses, ovary superior, round solid stem, yup)
